What I learned from the Social Network Analysis Class

Xinran Zhu
 2021-05-07  211 words   One minute 
Contents

Introduction

This semester, I took a course titled CI 8371 - Applied Social Network Analysis in Education taught by Prof. Bodong Chen. This course introduced a novel research methodology - Social Network Analysis (SNA) including the ethics, techniques, and application in education research. SNA is powerful to unveil the invisible yet important connections between communications, concepts and many other kinds of networks.

I am intriged of the word “connection”. In my life, no matter when I was watching a movie, or brainstorming about a design work, the most amazing moment is me startring to “see” new inspirations running to my head and connecting to my system. These moments always bring new ideas that make me feel so refreshed.

![knowledge-network]

Even more, SNA can capture the dynamics of a network. This is really powerful since interaction is never linear. Each piece of interaction can be interveined with each other.

“Individuals are embedded in thick webs of social relations and interactions” - Borgatti et al., 2009
